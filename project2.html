Brantford Transit Application


Project Details

There is currently general difficulty regarding wayfinding for posting secondary students who use the Brantford transit. Thus, we decided to focus this project on this topic. This project was done in a team of four third-year UX students. Everyone contributed to ideas and individual roles were also assigned. I contributed to the creation of the low fidelity paper prototype, as well as, playing a key role in creating the high fidelity prototype made in Adobe XD. I also contributed to the writing the reports.

Project Plan

We first started by creating a project plan outlining our target audience, goals, and plan for the study.



Target Audience

Post-secondary students who do not own a car

Goal

Increase the usability of the current Brantford transit app to improve wayfinding when taking the Brantford bus.

Plan

Gather user research through usability testing of prototypes


Exploratory Study

Interviewed 5 Wilfrid Laurier students who use the Brantford Bus system at least twice a week.

  • Two users noted that they were disoriented while using the Brantford bus app since the semantic cues in the app were difficult to understand.
  • We placed all the sketches on one table and voted on the design that incorporated our ideas the best.
  • They had difficulty getting to places quickly because of the way the information was presented in the app, lacking significant feedback in the visuospatial sketchpad.
  • Users also mentioned that they frequently forgot their OneCard.
With this knowledge, we thought that adding more perceptual cues and directional features would improve their transit experience.

Low fidelity Prototype

A paper prototype was created on cue card. The prototype embedded the OneCard into the transit app and attempts to make the directional feature as easy as possible to understand. A cognitive walkthrough approach used for the testing.

High Fidelity Prototype

From the feedback given a high-fidelity prototype was created in Adobe XD. Changes we made include adding a security feature for the one card, a bus stop near you page, and an option to add a password to view your one card.

A usability testing with using SAGAT was done on 5 more students. Students were given the task to find the setting pages as well as plan out a bus trip. All users were able to successfully complete all the tasks however, some took a longer than expected. It seemed like some of the icons made it confusing. In the SAGAT test, users were told to stop in the middle of a task and were asked how they got to where they were. All users were able to successfully answer the question showing good situation awareness.


👩

Feedback Given by users:

  • Add a homepage
  • Include a map in background of trip planner
  • Some icons were confusing
  • Add more colour

Final Prototype

A final prototype was created after the usability test. Some of the key changes we made included redoing some of the icons, adding text underneath the icons adding a home page, and adding a map in the background of trip planner.
