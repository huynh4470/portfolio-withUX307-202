Playground Count Video


Project Description

I worked for a professor to help create a video for her research project. She wanted to create a video on how to use a survey tool that collects data on schoolyards. This was a survey tool for kids to use.
My original role was to film and edit the full video by myself. In the end, the project was unsuccessful and I was terminated halfway through the project. The project turned out to be more complicated than originally imagined and I was lacking the skills set to complete the project. A big constraint that I face was the weather. The professor needed the video to be filmed outside. However, the only day that everyone was available was a very very cold and windy day in the winter. The wind caused background noise in the video making it hard to hear the dialogue. My camera stand also broke halfway through because it was so cold that the plastic snapped. Another constraint was that the actors were kids. They were awesome kids but they were young and lacked acting experience.

Reflection

This was a big learning experience for me. This was my first time filming in the winter. I learned that close up shots work best when there is background noise. I also learned to avoid bad weather conditions if possible.
